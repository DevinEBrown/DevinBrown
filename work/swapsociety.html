SwapSociety

UX Design & User Research

Project Overview

In today's world of increased online connectivity, there is a notable disconnection between individual interaction and local community engagement. Our Project outlines the development of a technology-based solution for facilitating bartering within local communities, aiming to understand user behaviors and foster connections.

Team: Devin Brown, Ana A., Chelsea C., Jennifer L., and Alyssa O.

My Role

Research

Competitive Analysis

Four competitors were identified for SWOT (strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats) analysis: Facebook, Craigslist, HaveNeed, and BarterChain. The direct competitors (HaveNeed and BarterChain) are both mobile applications that focus on bartering for goods and services. Craigslist and Facebook are indirect competitors that support bartering on their platforms.

Key Findings

Product Benefits
  • Provide a personalized local experience
  • Build a sense of community
  • Cost-effective alternative for locating local resources
Product Differenation
  • Build trust and establish connections by reducing anonymity
  • Bartering through in-person exchanges
  • Support community-building
